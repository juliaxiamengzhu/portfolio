I'm an aspiring UI Designer who grows bacteria by day...and then kill them with high pressure steam

Though I've always had an artistic streak, I majored in a food science degree where I learned a bit of of everything except for the arts. I currently work as a microbiologist in a manufacturing plant.

I wasn't just exposed to bacteria. I also witnessed how the elderly interact with technology. A task as simple as copying an Excel cell could distress them. This in turn help me empathize with the people who are left behind by technological advances.

I've also seen bad UX/UI designs, from testing labs' web pages to scientific software. Scientific software pose a unique challenge in that designers often don't have the background to understand scientists' needs. How can we expect otherwise experienced scientists to navigate through cumbersome menus if even younger scientists struggle with them?

Rather than be stuck with bad UI designs, I decided to embrace my artistic streak and pursue a designer career. Whatever I design, I keep in mind my experiences with coworkers struggling with bad UI, because everyone deserves a good user experience through good user interface.
